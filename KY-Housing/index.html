Kentucky Housing Ownership Patterns

Who owns and rents homes in 2010?

Data obtained through the U.S. Census Bureau. With assistance from my instructors at the University of Kentucky, New Maps Plus.

With the passage of the Fair Housing Law in 1968, Kentucky established a public policy of encouraging Fair treatment and equal housing opportunities for all persons, regardless of race, color, religion or national origin. The General Assembly stated that discrimination against an individual represents an affront to the dignity of the individual against whom it is directed, intensifies group conflicts, and is detrimental to the public health, safety, and welfare.

In 1972, the law was broadened by eliminating all major exemptions and thus covering virtually all housing in Kentucky. Discrimination in the public sale or rental of an individual home by the owner was prohibited. In 1980, the law was again broadened in order to prohibit sex discrimination in housing. In 1992, it again expanded to prohibit familial status and disability discrimination in housing.
